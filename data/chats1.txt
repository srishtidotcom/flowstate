[19/02/26, 2:24:32 PM] Malhar Deogoankar ( Pict ): 🚀 What is Dynamic Credit Scoring?

Traditional credit scores:

Calculated once at loan approval

Updated rarely (monthly/quarterly)

Based mostly on historical data

Dynamic scoring:

Continuously updates borrower risk

Uses recent behavioral patterns

Adapts to income/spending changes

👉 Instead of asking “Was this person good in the past?”
You ask: “How is this person behaving right now?”

🧠 Core Idea

Create a Behavioral Risk Engine that recalculates risk periodically (weekly/monthly) using rolling data windows.

Example:

Last 30 days transaction pattern

Last 3 months payment consistency

Income volatility in last 6 months

🔄 How It Would Work in Your System
Step 1: Initial Score (At Loan Approval)

Use full historical data

Predict Probability of Default (PD)

Convert to credit score

Step 2: Ongoing Monitoring (Post-Loan)

Every month:

Extract recent transactions

Compute updated features:

Income consistency index

Bill delay frequency

Credit utilization ratio

Cash flow volatility

Feed into trained model

Update PD

Adjust credit score

📊 Example

Borrower A:

Month 1 → Stable income → Score: 750

Month 3 → Missed 2 utility bills → Score drops to 710

Month 5 → Salary reduced → Score drops to 670

This allows early intervention before default happens.
